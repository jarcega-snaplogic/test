## Introduction to Solarpunk

### What is Solarpunk?

Solarpunk is a genre of speculative literature and art that envisions a planetary future in which humanity, non-human nature, and technology co-exist in harmony. At its core, Solarpunk marries:

- **"Solar"**: Representing renewable energy, environmental harmony, and sustainable technology
- **"Punk"**: Embracing DIY culture, countercultural movements, and resistance to oppressive societal structures

### Core Philosophy

Solarpunk visualizes a world detached from capitalist incentives, where humans use high tech and low tech in equal measures as tools for social and economic equality. Urban environments are designed to restore natural ecosystems threatened by climate change.

**Key Principle**: Solarpunk doesn't ignore climate issues, but focuses on how we make the world better, and how we thrive and adapt in the world we're headed into. It's an interruption of the narrative that we are doomed and there's nothing we can do about it.

### The "Punk" Ethos

The punk aspect implies:
- **Rebellion** against the dominant paradigm
- **DIY culture** and maker movements
- **Decentralization** and local decision-making
- **Mutual aid** and community cooperation
- **Anti-consumerism** and resource sharing
- **Egalitarianism** and social inclusiveness

## Visual Aesthetics

### Key Visual Elements

#### Technology + Nature Integration

The hallmark of solarpunk is the seamless integration of technology and nature:

- **Green architecture**: Towering vertical forests, rooftop gardens
- **Solar panels**: Stained-glass solar panels with art nouveau flourishes
- **Wind turbines**: Elegantly integrated into cityscapes
- **Living buildings**: Vegetation growing on and through structures
- **Water features**: Restored waterways, community fountains
- **Community spaces**: Shared gardens, maker spaces, tool libraries

#### Architectural Features

**Sustainable Materials:**
- Reclaimed wood
- Bamboo
- Mycelium (fungal root structures)
- Natural stone
- Recycled metals and glass

**Design Elements:**
- Sweeping biophilic shapes incorporating flora inside structures
- Vertical forests and green walls
- Energy-efficient windows
- Adaptive, modular buildings
- Frank Lloyd Wright's organic architecture influence

**Notable Real-World Example:**
- Bosco Verticale ("Vertical Forest") in Milan, Italy: Residential skyscrapers featuring about 100 vegetation species including trees, shrubs and plants on their exteriors

### Color Palettes

#### Primary Colors

Solarpunk typically utilizes:
- **Bright greens**: From lime to forest green, representing vegetation
- **Blues**: Sky blue, ocean blue, clean water
- **Natural earth tones**: Browns, tans, terracotta
- **Warm accents**: Sunset oranges, golden yellows (solar energy)
- **White and cream**: Clean technology, clouds, renewable materials

#### Design Approach

Color schemes celebrate:
- Eco-friendly design projects
- Garden landscapes
- Sustainable futures
- Dynamic range of earthy tones with lively greens and bright accents

### Art Nouveau Influence

The solarpunk visual identity is compared to Art Nouveau:
- **Plant depictions**: Organic, flowing botanical forms
- **Sinuous lines**: The "whiplash" curve
- **Integration of arts**: Applied arts into fine arts
- **Ornamental details**: Arts and Crafts movement influence
- **Decorative functionality**: Beauty serving purpose

### Representing Solarpunk in Pixel Art / 2D Games

#### Resolution & Constraints

Many successful indie games feature characters at lower resolutions (8-32 pixels tall), which works well for limited resources.

**Key Principle**: Make each pixel count. Limited pixels serve as a creative catalyst, forcing clarity and expressiveness.

#### Visual Strategies for Pixel Art

1. **Simplified Color Palettes**
   - Use 3-5 core greens for vegetation
   - 2-3 blues for water/sky
   - Earth tones for buildings
   - Bright accent colors for technology

2. **Iconic Vegetation Elements**
   - Small trees (3-5 pixels wide)
   - Vine patterns on buildings
   - Rooftop garden indicators
   - Green pixels scattered on structures

3. **Technology Integration**
   - Solar panel tiles (geometric patterns)
   - Wind turbine silhouettes
   - Glowing energy indicators
   - Recycling/composting stations

4. **Architectural Details**
   - Curved, organic building shapes
   - Mixed materials (wood texture patterns)
   - Community gathering spaces
   - Vertical garden columns

#### Animation Techniques

- **Idle animations**: Simple up/down movement (2-3 pixels)
- **Environmental animation**: Swaying plants, rotating turbines
- **Energy flow**: Glowing particle effects for solar/wind power
- **Water movement**: Simple ripple patterns

#### Character Expression in Limited Pixels

Even with limited resolution, convey emotion through:
- **Mouth changes**: Straight line → curved line for happiness/sadness
- **Eye variations**: Open, closed, surprised (1-2 pixel changes)
- **Body language**: Slight pose variations
- **Color shifts**: Subtle palette changes for mood

## Storytelling & Narrative Themes

### Core Narrative Themes

#### Hope as Central Theme

**Critical Distinction**: Rather than "if this goes on, here's the worst that can happen," solarpunk says, "if we change direction, here's a better version of what could happen."

Solarpunk is defined as a literary uprising of hope against the daily despair that environmental challenges present. Stories:
- Give us hope
- Inspire readers to act in ways that show respect for nature
- Help create societies that appreciate our indissoluble bond with life

**Important**: Apocalypse is the **starting point** for solarpunk futures, not the endpoint. Solarpunk is much more concerned with solutions and moving from apocalypse toward utopia.

#### Sustainability & Environmental Justice

Key themes include:
- **Environmental justice**: Climate issues are social justice issues
- **Community building**: Strong, interconnected communities
- **Sustainability**: Long-term thinking and regenerative practices
- **Collective action**: Mutual aid and cooperation
- **Inclusive decision-making**: Democratic processes

#### DIY & Maker Culture

Reflecting punk roots, solarpunk embraces:
- **Do-it-yourself ethic**: Self-sufficiency and skill-sharing
- **Local production**: Community workshops and makerspaces
- **Upcycled fashion**: Creative reuse of materials
- **Open-source culture**: Shared knowledge and tools
- **Tool libraries**: Community resource sharing

#### Technology Philosophy

Solarpunk promotes:
- **Appropriate technology**: Using high-tech and low-tech as needed
- **Convivial tools**: Technology that empowers users
- **Accessible technology**: Not excluding anyone
- **Regenerative systems**: Technology that heals rather than harms

### Character Archetypes

#### The Maker-Hero

The solarpunk archetype is often a "maker-hero" who:
- Has witnessed environmental disaster
- Experienced failures by central authorities
- Takes action to adapt to crises
- Defends nature and community
- Uses practical skills to solve problems

#### Diverse Representation

Solarpunk narration portrays:
- Characters from oppressed and marginalized groups
- People living more inclusively than they can today
- Diverse cultural origins and perspectives
- Different ages, abilities, and backgrounds
- Community-oriented rather than individualistic heroes

#### Character Roles in Solarpunk Settings

**Community Builders:**
- Urban farmers and permaculture specialists
- Teachers and knowledge sharers
- Organizers and consensus facilitators
- Artists and cultural workers

**Technical Specialists:**
- Renewable energy technicians
- Recycling and upcycling experts
- Engineers working on sustainable solutions
- Biotech researchers

**Healers & Caregivers:**
- Medical practitioners
- Mental health supporters
- Elders and wisdom keepers
- Environmental restoration workers

### Conflict Types in Solarpunk

Despite optimism, solarpunk needs engaging conflict:

#### Five Main Conflict Themes

1. **Character vs. Themselves**
   - Internal struggles with old habits
   - Learning to trust community over individualism
   - Overcoming trauma from "before times"
   - Balancing personal desires with community needs

2. **Character vs. Another Character**
   - Disagreements on best approaches
   - Resource allocation debates
   - Different visions for the future
   - Personal conflicts within cooperative structures

3. **Character vs. Their Environment**
   - Remnants of climate damage
   - Restoring damaged ecosystems
   - Adapting to changed conditions
   - Limited resources requiring creativity

4. **Character vs. Nature**
   - Extreme weather events
   - Ecosystem restoration challenges
   - Working with nature's timelines
   - Unexpected consequences of interventions

5. **Character vs. Machine/System**
   - Dealing with remnants of old systems
   - Technology that doesn't serve community
   - Repurposing harmful infrastructure
   - Creating new systems to replace broken ones

#### Balancing Optimism with Conflict

**Important Principle**: Good conflict is about values. If your character has no values, you have no character.

**Solarpunk-Specific Approaches:**
- Conflicts arise from dealing with remnants of old systems
- New problems arise even in better societies
- Focus on problem-solving rather than destruction
- Character-focused conflict within worldbuilding context
- Solutions involve community and cooperation

**Tonal Range:**
- Can be utopian
- Can be optimistic
- Can show struggles en route to better world
- **Never** dystopian

**"Dark Solarpunk"**: Some works explore "less naively optimistic" versions while maintaining hope as the ultimate direction.

### Storytelling as Activism

The power of solarpunk storytelling:
- Uses collective imagination against climate emergency
- Provides counter-narratives to dystopian fiction
- Inspires and mobilizes individuals toward action
- Fosters critical hope
- Makes better futures feel achievable

## Character Development

### Creating Memorable NPCs

#### The Four Key Questions

When developing any NPC, ask:
1. **Who is this NPC?**
2. **What do they think of the player?**
3. **What do they want?**
4. **How are they feeling?**

#### Personality Foundation

**A personality is a combination of:**
- Traits
- Emotions
- Attitudes
- Values
- Behaviors

**Practical Approach**: Begin with 3-5 adjectives that describe the character:
- Boisterous
- Stingy
- Scared
- Suave
- Hopeful
- Pragmatic
- Nurturing

#### Writing Character Voice

**Critical Principle**: Each character acts only according to their personality, history, and current situation. Never write dialogue that strays from the character's voice.

**Voice Elements:**
- **Vocabulary**: What words do they use?
- **Sentence structure**: Short and punchy? Long and flowing?
- **Speech patterns**: Formal? Casual? Technical?
- **Catchphrases or quirks**: Unique verbal tics
- **What they talk about**: Their interests and concerns

#### Making NPCs Memorable with Limited Resources

**You don't need much dialogue to give characters personality:**

1. **Use Contrast**
   - In a town full of frightened NPCs, one who's excited stands out
   - Descriptive contrasting short-hands pique interest

2. **Show Conflict**
   - Interesting NPCs have internal or external conflict
   - Even minor characters can hint at deeper stories

3. **Consistency**
   - Make a few clear decisions about the character
   - Write to those decisions every time
   - Players will remember consistency

4. **Distinct Dialogue Styles**
   - Different speech patterns
   - Unique perspectives
   - Specific knowledge or interests

### Character Development Through Humor

#### Humor in Hopeful Sci-Fi

Humor serves crucial functions:
- **Humanizes characters**: Makes them relatable despite fantastical settings
- **Bridges the gap**: Between the familiar and the strange
- **Lightens heavy themes**: Balances serious topics with levity
- **Shows personality**: How characters react to absurdity

#### Classic Example: Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

Despite Douglas Adams' cynicism, the hopeful message: "With determination, a positive mindset, and highly improbable luck, you can fix anything."

**Key Techniques:**
- Absurd situations
- Memorable characters
- Sharp wit
- Relatable human reactions

#### Creating Comedy in Sci-Fi

**Character-Driven Humor:**
- Characters in exaggerated or absurd situations
- Flawed but relatable protagonists
- Reactions and interactions drive comedy
- Characters need clear motivations even in silly scenarios

**Examples from Recent Games:**
- **Waypoint Kangaroo**: Quirky, vaguely incompetent super-hero spy
- **Becky Chambers' Wayfarers Series**: Charming cast committed to saving the world
- Cozy sci-fi with optimistic characters and hopeful futures

### Character Interaction Systems

#### Party Banter Best Practices

**Dragon Age Model:**
- Banter plays when passing certain points
- Randomized based on present characters
- Over 5 hours of voiced party banter (Inquisition)
- Characters comment on story and locations
- Develops relationships between companions

**Mass Effect Evolution:**
- ME1/2: Conversations at trigger points (elevators, hub areas)
- ME3: Increased natural dialogue during missions
- Citadel DLC: Characters very chatty in/out of combat
- ME Andromeda: Even more interaction on and off ship

**Key Principles:**
- Characters should talk to each other, not just the player
- Unique personalities reacting to each other
- Comments on current situations and locations
- Both serious and lighthearted exchanges
- Develops companion relationships organically

#### BioWare and Obsidian Approach

Industry standard for character interaction:
- Rich banter systems
- Companion approval/disapproval
- Characters remember player choices
- Relationships evolve over time
- Special dialogue for specific party compositions

### Character Relationship Mechanics

#### Friendship Systems

**Basic Structure:**
- Dialogue choices shape friendships over time
- Measured in points or hearts
- Strong bonds unlock:
  - Party invites
  - Battle perks
  - Backstory reveals
  - Special events

**Progressive Systems:**
- Some paths locked until prerequisites met
- Example: Romance requires sufficient friendship first
- Natural progression feels more authentic

#### Relationship Benefits

**Narrative Benefits:**
- Deeper character understanding
- Trust-based story branches
- Collaborative problem-solving
- Character growth moments

**Mechanical Benefits (if desired):**
- Bonus dice for helping relationship partners
- Stress reduction through positive interactions
- Unlocked abilities or team moves
- Better outcomes in specific situations

**Tabletop RPG Inspiration:**
- Characters build relationship strength
- Grant bonuses when helping each other
- Romantic activities can reduce stress
- Relationships can break if mistreated

## RPG Narrative Systems

### Dialogue Writing Fundamentals

#### Core Principles

1. **Every line should serve purpose**
   - Reveals character personality
   - Advances the plot
   - Provides important information
   - Builds world atmosphere

2. **Concise and impactful**
   - Avoid long lines
   - Omit needless words
   - Keep important information
   - Respect player's time

3. **Character-driven**
   - NPCs with unique personalities
   - Distinct backstories
   - Clear motivations
   - Authentic reactions

#### Avoiding Boring NPCs

**Problem**: NPCs with little character create boring worlds
**Solution**: Give every NPC:
- Unique personality
- Backstory (even if players never learn all of it)
- Motivations
- Opinions about current events
- Relationship to other NPCs

### Dialogue Systems & Structures

#### Dialogue Trees

**Definition**: Gameplay mechanic where players choose what to say, making subsequent choices until conversation ends.

**Basic Structure:**
- Branching paths
- Multiple response options
- Consequences for choices
- Potential state tracking

**Technical Implementation:**
- Scriptable Objects (Unity)
- JSON data structures
- External dialogue files
- Node-based systems

#### Flow Patterns

**Mass Effect Approach:**
- Present options before NPC finishes
- Player makes decision
- Avatar delivers response
- Little/no pause in conversation
- **Benefit**: More natural flow

**Traditional Approach:**
- NPC speaks completely
- Player reads options
- Player selects response
- Avatar responds
- **Benefit**: Player has time to consider

#### Dialogue Choice UI Patterns

**Common Presentations:**
1. **Word-for-word**: Show exact player line
2. **Paraphrased**: Summary of intent
3. **Tone-based**: Emotional approach (diplomatic, aggressive, humorous)
4. **Icon-based**: Visual representations

**Best Practices:**
- Clear indication of what choice means
- Consistent presentation
- Appropriate pacing
- Visual hierarchy for importance

### Party Banter Implementation

#### System Architecture

**Components Needed:**
1. **Trigger system**: Location-based or time-based
2. **Condition checking**: Which characters present
3. **State tracking**: What's been said before
4. **Priority system**: Story-relevant vs. ambient
5. **Cooldown timers**: Prevent spam

**Data Structure Example:**
```
BanterEvent {
  id: unique_identifier
  characters: [character_a, character_b]
  conditions: [location, story_flag, relationship_level]
  priority: 1-10
  cooldown: time_in_seconds
  dialogue: [
    {speaker: character_a, line: "text"},
    {speaker: character_b, line: "text"}
  ]
}
```

#### Implementation Tips

1. **Randomization with weights**
   - Story-important banter: Higher priority
   - Ambient banter: Lower priority
   - Recently played: Reduce weight

2. **Context awareness**
   - Check current location
   - Check recent story events
   - Check character relationships
   - Check party composition

3. **Interruption handling**
   - Save state if combat starts
   - Option to skip long banter
   - Resume vs. cancel logic

## Web-Based Implementation

### Dialog Box Design

#### HTML5 Native Dialog Element

**Modern Approach**: Use `<dialog>` tag
- Native browser support
- Semantic HTML
- Built-in modal behavior
- Accessible by default

**Structure:**
```html
<dialog id="game-dialog">
  <div class="speaker-name">Character Name</div>
  <div class="message">Dialog text goes here...</div>
  <div class="footer">
    <button>Continue</button>
  </div>
</dialog>
```

**Browser Support Considerations:**
- Check current support status
- Consider polyfills for older browsers
- Progressive enhancement approach

#### Dialog Box Components

**Three Main Parts:**
1. **Speaker identifier**: Name, portrait, or both
2. **Message area**: The actual dialogue text
3. **Footer**: Continue button, choices, etc.

**Best Practices:**
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Readable fonts (14-18px minimum)
- Sufficient contrast
- Mobile-friendly sizing
- Consistent positioning

#### Styling Considerations

**Backdrop:**
- Semi-transparent overlay (rgba(0,0,0,0.5))
- Blurs game world (optional)
- Click-outside behavior

**Box Design:**
- Border radius for friendliness (solarpunk aesthetic)
- Natural color palette
- Adequate padding
- Box shadow for depth

**Accessibility:**
- Keyboard navigation support
- Screen reader compatibility
- Focus management
- Skip/speed-up options

### Text Rendering & Animation

#### Typewriter Effect

**Why Use It:**
- Creates sense of character speaking
- Controls reading pace
- Adds life to static text
- Classic RPG feel

**CSS-Only Approach:**
```css
.typewriter {
  overflow: hidden;
  border-right: .15em solid green;
  white-space: nowrap;
  animation:
    typing 3.5s steps(40, end),
    blink-caret .75s step-end infinite;
}

@keyframes typing {
  from { width: 0 }
  to { width: 100% }
}
```

**JavaScript Approach:**
```javascript
function typeWriter(element, text, speed = 50) {
  let i = 0;
  function type() {
    if (i < text.length) {
      element.innerHTML += text.charAt(i);
      i++;
      setTimeout(type, speed);
    }
  }
  type();
}
```

**Advanced Features:**
- Variable speed (slower for emphasis)
- Pause on punctuation
- Sound effects per character
- Skip/instant-complete option
- Different speeds per character type

#### Performance Optimization

**For long dialogue:**
- Use requestAnimationFrame
- Batch DOM updates
- Pre-calculate timing
- Efficient string manipulation

### Portrait System

#### Visual Novel Sprite Approach

**Basic Concept:**
- "Standing image" (立ち絵, tachie)
- Shown from thighs/waist up
- Changes expression based on mood
- Static but with variations

**Layered System:**

**Base Setup:**
1. **Base layer**: Body and outfit
2. **Expression layer**: Face, eyes, mouth
3. **Additional layers**: Accessories, effects

**Advanced Layering:**
- Separate eyebrows from mouth
- More expression combinations
- Fewer total assets needed
- Example: 5 mouths × 5 eyebrows = 25 expressions

**File Organization:**
```
/portraits/
  /character_name/
    base.png
    /expressions/
      happy.png
      sad.png
      surprised.png
      angry.png
    /poses/
      neutral.png
      pointing.png
      crossed_arms.png
```

#### Implementation for Web

**CSS Layering:**
```css
.portrait-container {
  position: relative;
  width: 200px;
  height: 300px;
}

.portrait-layer {
  position: absolute;
  top: 0;
  left: 0;
  width: 100%;
  height: 100%;
}
```

**JavaScript Control:**
```javascript
function setExpression(character, expression) {
  const expressionLayer = document.querySelector(
    `.portrait-layer.expression`
  );
  expressionLayer.src = `portraits/${character}/${expression}.png`;
}
```

**Extended Set:**
- Confused
- Embarrassed
- Determined
- Worried
- Smiling
- Laughing
- Crying

**Solarpunk-Specific Expressions:**
- Hopeful
- Thoughtful

### Data Structure & Management

#### External Data Files

**Best Practice**: Store dialogue in external files
- JSON for structure
- Markdown for readability
- Easy editing without code changes
- Translation-friendly

**Example JSON Structure:**
```json
{
  "scene_id": "garden_meeting_01",
  "characters": ["maya", "kai"],
  "dialogue": [
    {
      "speaker": "maya",
      "expression": "excited",
      "text": "Look at this! The tomatoes are finally fruiting!",
      "choices": null
    },
    {
      "speaker": "kai",
      "expression": "happy",
      "text": "The new trellis design is working perfectly.",
      "choices": null
    },
    {
      "speaker": "maya",
      "expression": "thoughtful",
      "text": "Should we share the design with the other gardens?",
      "choices": [
        {
          "text": "Absolutely, knowledge should be shared!",
          "next": "sharing_knowledge_path",
          "relationship_change": {"kai": 5}
        },
        {
          "text": "Let's perfect it first.",
          "next": "cautious_path",
          "relationship_change": {"kai": -2}
        }
      ]
    }
  ]
}
```

#### State Management

**What to Track:**
- Current scene/dialogue node
- Character relationships
- Story flags/variables
- Choices made
- Seen dialogue
- Character locations

**LocalStorage for Web:**
```javascript
// Save game state
function saveState(gameState) {
  localStorage.setItem('gameState',
    JSON.stringify(gameState));
}

// Load game state
function loadState() {
  const saved = localStorage.getItem('gameState');
  return saved ? JSON.parse(saved) : defaultState;
}
```

### Responsive Design

#### Mobile Considerations

**Dialog Box:**
- Full width on small screens

### Narrative Structure Recommendations

   - Who's involved?
   - What's the emotional tone?

   - Don't worry about length first
   - Capture personality

   - Ensure clarity

   - Is personality clear?
